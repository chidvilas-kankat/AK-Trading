### 2 · Problem / Agitation (reframed as *misconception*, not pain)
> "Most people approach the market in one of two ways — and both fail.
> The first treats it like a **casino**: random bets, tips, gut calls, hoping to get lucky. The second treats it like **magic**: searching for the one secret indicator that prints money.
>
> Neither is how professionals actually operate. A surgeon doesn't 'feel lucky'. An auditor doesn't 'wing it'. They followed a structured education, built a method, and earned the right to practise. Trading is no different — and approaching a serious financial skill as a gamble or a shortcut is precisely why ~9 in 10 lose."

### 3 · Reframe / Opportunity
> "**Successfully trading the market is part science and part art. The science, you can learn. The art comes with disciplined experience.** That's the whole philosophy here.
>
> The science is teachable and structured: how markets work, risk and position sizing, reading price, building a plan, managing yourself. The art — judgement, patience, temperament — develops as you apply the science honestly over time. Our job is to give you a rigorous foundation in the science and a real framework to develop the art — the way a professional school would, not the way a 60‑second ad does."

### 4 · Named Methodology — **The AK Practitioner Curriculum**
**Objective:** Make the education feel *credential‑grade* and progressive.

Frame it as a **structured progression, beginner → practitioner**, like a real diploma:
- **Foundation** — market structure, instruments, how trading really works.
- **Core discipline** — risk management and position sizing (the professional's first skill).
- **Technique** — reading price and structure (on historical data).
- **Method** — building and back‑testing a rules‑based plan.
- **Mastery & temperament** — psychology, journaling, continuous review.

> Microcopy: *"Built like a course, not a webinar funnel: progressive modules, clear outcomes, and a focus on competence you can demonstrate — not a single 'secret' you'll forget by Friday."*

### 7 · Curriculum (diploma‑style accordion, outcome‑led)
1. **Foundations of the market** — instruments, mechanics, cash vs F&O, why most lose.
2. **Risk management** — the professional's first skill: sizing, R‑multiples, drawdown control.
3. **Reading price** — trend, structure, support/resistance (historical case studies).
4. **Building a trading plan** — rules, entries, exits, back‑testing the idea.
5. **Trading psychology & temperament** — discipline as a professional habit.
6. **Review & continuous improvement** — journaling, the practitioner's loop.
7. **Capstone** — assemble and present your own written method (educational).

> Add a line on **certification/recognition** *only if real* (e.g. "certificate of completion"; NSE Academy/NISM pathway). Never imply a credential you don't issue.

## Version‑specific UI/UX direction

- **Accent strategy:** shift from the bright base green to a more **academic, institutional palette** — a deep **teal/forest** primary with a restrained **brass/gold** accent for "credential" cues (badges, the certificate motif, section rules). Reserve gold strictly for trust/credential elements; keep CTAs a single confident colour.
  - Suggested: `--accent: #0E6E63` (deep teal) · `--credential: #C8A24B` (brass) · base navy/paper from Master §5.
- **Mood:** calm, premium, "respected institution" — generous white space, hairline rules, serif accents allowed.
- **Type:** pair a **serif display** (e.g. **Fraunces** or **Source Serif 4**) for headlines with **Inter/IBM Plex Sans** body — the serif signals education/authority and sets this version apart from the all‑sans others.
- **Hero composition:** centred or left, teaching/credential imagery; a slim "as taught with [cert]" badge row beneath the trust line.
- **Imagery direction:** real teaching, real workbook, real certificate — institutional, not lifestyle.
- **Micro‑interactions:** restrained — gentle fades, an underline‑draw on links; nothing flashy (flashiness undercuts the credibility positioning).
- **Mobile:** sticky CTA; curriculum reads as a clean numbered syllabus; certificate motif sits near the founder block.

## Version‑specific build notes
- If you offer a completion certificate, build a simple **certificate template** and mention verifiability — strong trust asset for this audience.
- Surface the **NISM/NSE pathway** as a dedicated small section or FAQ — it's a differentiator that lands hardest with this segment.

## Compliance flags (this version)
- "Profession/skill/certificate" language is the *most* compliant framing — but **never invent accreditation.** State exactly what you issue and your real NISM/NSE status.
- Avoid any drift into "career income" implications; the promise is *competence and a credential‑grade education*, not earnings.
- Keep all teaching examples ≥3 months old.
