### 2 · Problem / Agitation
> "Every trading ad you see assumes you're free from 9:15 to 3:30, glued to a screen, ready to scalp. So you've quietly concluded trading isn't for you — because you have a *job*.
>
> Or worse: someone convinced you to try day‑trading on your lunch break, you got chopped up by every small move, and you decided you 'don't have the time or the temperament'. The truth is you were sold the wrong *style* of trading for your life. Intraday demands constant attention you don't have. There's another way that doesn't — and almost nobody teaches it to people like you."

### 3 · Reframe / Opportunity
> "Meet **swing trading**: holding positions for several days to a few weeks, based on a plan you build in the evening when the market's closed and your head is clear.
>
> No live screen‑watching. No quitting your job. No adrenaline‑soaked scalping. You do your analysis after dinner, set your rules and alerts, and check in for a few minutes a day. It rewards patience and process over reflexes and screen‑time — which is *perfect* for a working professional. You keep your salary (your safety net) while you build the skill, on your schedule."

### 4 · Named Methodology — **The Evening‑Trader System**
**Objective:** Prove it genuinely fits a workday — make the *time commitment* concrete.

A weekly rhythm any employed person can actually keep:
- **Weekend (60–90 min):** scan and shortlist; build your watchlist for the week.
- **Weekday evening (15–20 min):** review the day's closes, set entries/exits and alerts for tomorrow.
- **During the day (≈0 min):** alerts do the watching; pre‑set orders do the executing.
- **Weekly review (30 min):** journal, learn, refine.

> Microcopy: *"Total time: a few focused hours a week, all outside market hours. No screen‑staring, no boss noticing, no career risk."*

## Version‑specific UI/UX direction

- **Accent strategy:** warm, balanced, "life‑friendly" — keep the trustworthy navy base but pair it with a **warm secondary** (soft amber/terracotta) and lots of light, airy space. The vibe is "calm evening at home", not "trading desk".
  - Suggested: base navy/paper from Master §5 + `--warm: #E8A87C` accent for highlights; CTAs single clear colour.
- **Signature motif: time.** A subtle clock/calendar/weekly‑rhythm graphic recurs — especially the §4 weekly schedule, which should be a clean visual timeline (Weekend → Evenings → Weekly review).
- **Type:** friendly, humanist — **Plus Jakarta Sans** headlines, **Inter** body; comfortable, not corporate‑cold.
- **Hero composition:** relatable after‑hours imagery; a small "≈15 min/day, outside market hours" badge near the CTA is a strong objection‑killer.
- **Imagery direction:** real working professionals, home/evening settings, phones — *not* multi‑monitor battle stations.
- **Micro‑interactions:** gentle; the weekly‑rhythm timeline can build left‑to‑right on scroll.
- **Mobile:** sticky "Save My Evening Seat →"; the weekly schedule becomes a vertical timeline; emphasise that sessions are recorded.

## Version‑specific build notes
- Make the **weekly‑rhythm timeline** a clean reusable graphic — it's the proof that this fits a job, and the single most persuasive asset for this audience.
- Consider an **evening time‑slot** prominently displayed for the masterclass (e.g. 8–9:30 PM) — congruent and conversion‑boosting.
- A small "time you'll spend per week" calculator/graphic reinforces the low‑commitment promise.

## Compliance flags (this version)
- **Be especially careful** not to frame trading as a salary replacement or "passive income" — that's both a performance claim and ethically risky. The promise is a *skill that fits your schedule*, with your job as the safety net.
- "Manage in minutes a day" describes *time*, not ease of profit — keep that distinction clean.
- All setups/examples ≥3 months old.
