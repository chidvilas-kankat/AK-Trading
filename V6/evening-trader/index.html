The honest problem

Every trading ad assumes you're free from 9:15 to 3:30.

Glued to a screen, ready to scalp. So you've quietly concluded trading isn't for you — because you have a job.

Or worse: someone convinced you to day-trade on your lunch break, you got chopped up by every small move, and you decided you "don't have the time or the temperament."

The truth is you were sold the wrong style of trading for your life. Intraday demands constant attention you don't have. There's another way that doesn't — and almost nobody teaches it to people like you.

What you've been told vs. the truth

  • "You must watch screens all day."
    Built for intraday scalpers — not someone in back-to-back meetings.

  • "You must quit your job to be serious."
    Reckless advice that throws away your safety net.

  • The truth: there's a calmer, slower style designed to be managed outside market hours.

Meet swing trading

Plan it in the evening. Manage it in minutes.

Swing trading means holding positions for several days to a few weeks, based on a plan you build in the evening — when the market's closed and your head is clear. You do your analysis after dinner, set your rules and alerts, and check in for a few minutes a day.

It rewards patience and process over reflexes and screen-time — which is perfect for a working professional. You keep your salary, your safety net, while you build the skill, on your schedule.

No live screen-watching

Alerts do the watching. Pre-set orders do the executing while you're at work.

No quitting your job
