### 4 · Named Methodology — **The Honest Method**
**Objective:** Show there's real substance behind the stance (so it's not just attitude).

Frame your teaching as deliberately the opposite of the hype playbook:
- **Independence over dependence** — you leave able to trade on your own.
- **Risk before reward** — capital preservation taught first.
- **Process over predictions** — rules and a written plan, not tips.
- **Honesty over hype** — the odds and the disclaimers are front‑and‑centre, not buried.

> Microcopy: *"A method you can take with you — not a subscription you're trapped in."*

## Version‑specific UI/UX direction

- **Accent strategy:** high contrast and a touch of edge. Keep navy/white base; introduce a **bold signal colour** (a strong amber or a confident red‑orange) used for the "crossed‑out hype" and the manifesto emphasis. CTAs stay the single AK green so the action is unmistakable.
- **Signature motif: the "Them vs Us" comparison.** It appears as the hero split *and* the full table in §3 *and* a recurring two‑column rhythm. Strike‑through styling on the "them" column.
- **Type:** display **Space Grotesk** 800 or a slightly condensed bold (declarative, manifesto energy); body **Inter**.
- **Tone of layout:** punchy, confident, lots of short bold lines and statement type. Pull‑quotes set large.
- **Hero composition:** left manifesto headline, right the crossed‑out hype panel; on mobile, the hype panel sits above, visibly struck through, then the clean "us" statement.
- **Micro‑interactions:** the strike‑through on "them" items can draw in on scroll; the comparison rows can slide in alternately (them from left greyed, us from right in brand colour).
- **Mobile:** sticky CTA; the comparison table becomes stacked paired cards (grey "them" card → green‑accented "us" card).

## Version‑specific build notes
- The **comparison table** is the hero asset — build it as a responsive component (table on desktop, stacked pairs on mobile).
- Add a small, genuinely useful **"scam red‑flags checklist"** as a lead magnet — it's perfectly on‑theme and builds trust fast.

## Compliance flags (this version)
- Naming "fake gurus / signal sellers" as a *category* is fine; **do not name or defame specific real people or companies** — keep the villain generic ("the hype industry"), avoid legal exposure.
- Resist the temptation to imply "we're honest, so we're safer to *profit* with" — honesty is the differentiator, not a veiled performance claim.
- The "spot a scam" module is a great asset *and* a compliance signal — keep it educational and accurate.
