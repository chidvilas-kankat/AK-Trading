## Version‑specific UI/UX direction

- **Accent strategy:** the **warmest, friendliest** palette in the pack — soft, approachable, low‑intimidation. Keep a trustworthy base but lean into gentle colour and rounded shapes; avoid dense charts and aggressive red/green tickers that scare beginners.
  - Suggested: softer paper backgrounds, a friendly **teal/green** primary, warm accent; rounded 20px cards; generous spacing.
- **Signature motif: steps/ladder.** A clear "Step 1 → 2 → 3" visual language throughout; the §4 Ladder is a literal, friendly staircase/progress graphic.
- **Type:** rounded, friendly, highly legible — **Plus Jakarta Sans** or **Nunito Sans** headlines; **Inter / Noto Sans** body with **Noto Sans Devanagari / Noto Sans Kannada** for vernacular. Slightly larger base size (17–18px) for comfort.
- **Hero composition:** warm welcoming imagery; the "no jargon / your language" badges sit near the CTA.
- **Imagery & icons:** friendly, simple, illustrative icons; the mentor looking approachable; avoid intimidating six‑monitor setups entirely.
- **Plain‑language everywhere:** define any unavoidable term inline (tooltip or parenthesis). This is a UX *principle* for this version, not just copy.
- **Micro‑interactions:** gentle, encouraging; the Ladder can fill in as you scroll, giving a sense of progress.
- **Mobile:** sticky "Start From Zero →"; the Ladder is a friendly vertical staircase; offer a clear language toggle if you publish vernacular variants.

## Version‑specific build notes
- **Vernacular variants:** strongly consider Hindi and Kannada versions of this page (and the masterclass). Use proper Devanagari/Kannada fonts; don't auto‑translate copy — have a native speaker write it.
- An **interactive glossary** (tap a term → plain‑language definition) is a perfect on‑theme feature and lead magnet.
- Keep the page visually light and uncluttered — every extra chart or number raises the intimidation barrier you're trying to remove.

## Compliance flags (this version)
- Beginners are the **most vulnerable** audience, so be *extra* careful: no income framing, no "start earning", emphasise risk and that ~9 in 10 lose, and that beginners should learn before risking real money.
- Vernacular copy must carry the **same disclaimers** as English — don't let the translated version drop the compliance language.
- All examples ≥3 months old and clearly educational; keep charts simple and labelled.
