01 — The honest truth

This isn't a knowledge gap.
It's an execution gap.

Let's be honest about your situation. You've watched the videos. You can name the candlestick patterns. You know what support and resistance are. On a chart from last week, you can point to exactly where you "should" have entered and exited.

"And yet — in real time, with real money, your heart pounding — you enter late, you move your stop, you hold losers and cut winners, you revenge-trade after a red day."

02 — The reframe

Trading is a performance skill — not a knowledge subject.

It's more like learning to drive than learning history. You didn't become a driver by watching driving videos — you became one through supervised, repeated practice until the right actions became automatic.

That's the whole problem with how trading is taught in India: it's all what and almost no how. We teach the opposite way — built entirely around execution, repetition, review and the psychology of doing the right thing when it's hard.

How it's taught

99%
  • Watch a 2-hour video
  • Memorise patterns
  • Take a quiz
  • Repeat, alone, on real money

How skill is built

us
  • Concept introduced, then drilled
  • Rehearse in no-risk environment
  • Execute by a strict checklist
  • Review every decision in a journal
  • Loop — until it's automatic

03 — The methodology

The AK Execution Loop.

A continuous 5-stage cycle that compounds with every iteration. Concepts are step one of five. Most courses stop there. The other four steps — where skill is actually built — are the whole point of this one.

The loop repeats. Skill compounds.

01

Knowledge

The concept, taught properly — using historical examples, not last-night's hot-take. You learn why a pattern works, not just what to call it.

02

Practice

Rehearse the setup in a no-risk environment — replay tools, paper trades, screenshare drills — until the action becomes familiar before money is ever on the line.

03

Implementation

Execute the process by a strict, written checklist. No improvising. The pre-trade checklist removes the spaces where hesitation and FOMO live.

04

Analyse & Improve

Journal every decision — not the P&L. We hunt the leak: a checklist item you skipped, a stop you nudged, an emotion you didn't name. Then you patch it.

05

Control

The discipline systems — pre-market routines, breathing protocols, position-size guards — that make you follow your own rules when the market is screaming at you not to.

Then back to Knowledge — sharper, with a new lesson banked. That's the loop.

04 — Why this exists

"I understood the market for years before I could actually trade it. That gap is exactly why I built this."

I'm Abul. I spent six years reading every book on technical analysis I could find, watched thousands of hours of content, and could explain Wyckoff and Elliott to anyone who asked. I still blew up two accounts in 2014–15. Not because I didn't know what to do — but because in the moment, I couldn't do it.

What finally changed things wasn't another book. It was a discipline system from sport — daily reps, video review, journaling, accountability — applied to trading. Within 18 months, my behaviour metrics (entry timing, stop integrity, plan adherence) shifted before my P&L did. The P&L followed because the behaviour did.

08 — Frequently asked

Straight answers.
No softening.

The four questions we get most often, answered plainly. If yours isn't here, write to us — we answer every email.

"I already know a lot of theory — is this too basic for me?"

If you know the theory but still lose, this is built for exactly you. We start where the videos stop: execution. Week one will feel almost insultingly simple. By week three, you'll see why we started there.

"Will you give signals or buy/sell calls?"

No. The entire point is to make you execute your own decisions, not depend on ours. Signal services breed dependency; we breed independence. We also operate as a registered Research Analyst — we follow SEBI guidelines and do not provide buy/sell recommendations as part of this course.

"How is this different from a free YouTube channel?"

YouTube delivers knowledge. Skill is built through structured practice, review, and accountability — which is everything this programme is and nothing YouTube can be. If watching could make you a trader, the comments section would be a hall of fame instead of a graveyard.

"Do you guarantee profits?"

No. We train the process. Outcomes depend on you, market conditions, and many factors outside our control. About 9 in 10 retail derivatives traders lose money. Our job is to make you one of the disciplined few — not to promise anything beyond that. Anyone who guarantees you trading profits is either lying or hasn't been trading long enough.

"How much time per week does this require?"

Plan for ~4 hours: one 90-minute live session, two 60-minute self-practice drills, and 30 minutes of journaling spread across the week. Recordings cover you if you miss a live session — but the live drills are where the real work happens.

"Is this only for F&O traders?"

No. The Execution Loop is instrument-agnostic — equity intraday, swing, options, even crypto if that's your thing. We use Nifty/Bank Nifty for most demonstrations because that's what most Indian retail traders are in, but the framework transfers.
