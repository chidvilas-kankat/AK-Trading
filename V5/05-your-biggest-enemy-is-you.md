### 3 · Reframe / Opportunity
> "Here's the uncomfortable, liberating truth: **the biggest edge in trading isn't a strategy — it's the discipline to follow one.** A mediocre method executed with iron discipline beats a brilliant method sabotaged by emotion, every single time.
>
> That's why professionals obsess over psychology and risk, while beginners obsess over entries. The good news? Emotional discipline is a *trainable* skill. There are specific systems — checklists, rules, journaling, routines — that take the decision out of the heat of the moment and put it where it belongs: in cold, pre‑committed logic. That's what we teach."

### 4 · Named Methodology — **The Calm Trader Framework** (4 traps → 4 systems)
**Objective:** Name the enemy, then hand over the cure for each.

| The mental trap | What it costs you | The system that fixes it |
|---|---|---|
| **FOMO** (chasing) | Late entries, bad prices | A pre‑trade checklist that only green‑lights *your* setup |
| **Fear** (hesitation / moving stops) | Missed valid trades, ballooning losses | Pre‑committed, rules‑based exits decided *before* you enter |
| **Greed** (over‑sizing, no exit) | One trade wrecks the month | Fixed position sizing + a written profit/exit rule |
| **Revenge** (tilt after a loss) | Small loss → disaster | A "circuit‑breaker" routine: stop, log, step away |

> Microcopy: *"You can't delete emotion. You *can* build systems so emotion never gets to make the decision."*

## Version‑specific UI/UX direction

- **Accent strategy:** mute the palette into a **calm, behavioural** scheme — soft slate, warm sand/oatmeal, a single grounded **deep‑indigo or sage** accent. This is the *least* "trading‑floor" of all versions on purpose: calm signals control.
  - Suggested: `--calm-bg: #F4F2EC` (warm sand) · `--accent: #3B5B7A` (deep slate‑blue) or `#5E7C6B` (sage) · ink/paper from Master §5. CTAs remain a single clear colour.
- **Mood:** spacious, slow, considered — large line‑heights, generous margins, breathing room. The *whitespace itself* communicates composure.
- **Type:** a **humanist serif or a soft humanist sans** for headlines (e.g. **Newsreader**, **Fraunces** soft, or **Epilogue**); body **Inter** at a comfortable 17–18px with 1.7 line‑height.
- **Hero composition:** centred, calm; the "noisy mind vs calm mind" motif understated. Avoid red/green flashing numbers anywhere — they're tonally wrong here.
- **Signature motif:** the **4‑traps → 4‑systems** table, presented as calm paired cards.
- **Micro‑interactions:** slow, soft fades (250–350ms); a subtle "settle" animation rather than anything snappy. Strongly respect reduced‑motion.
- **Mobile:** sticky CTA; the traps table becomes four calm stacked cards, each trap paired with its fix.

## Version‑specific build notes
