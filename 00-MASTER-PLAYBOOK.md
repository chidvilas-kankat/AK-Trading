# AK Trading Academy — Landing Page Playbook Pack
## Master Playbook · Research, Shared Foundations & How to Use the 10 Versions

*Prepared mid‑2026. This is the spine of a 11‑file pack: this master plus **10 distinct landing‑page playbooks** (`01`–`10`). Each version has its own hook, big idea, framing, storytelling and visual direction — cloned (structurally, never word‑for‑word) from a study of 30+ real trading academies. The **founder/mentor block, design system, build stack and compliance rules are shared and live here** so each version can stay focused on what makes it different. Marketing/strategy document — **not legal or investment advice**; validate every claim and the regulatory sections with a SEBI‑aware securities lawyer before you publish.*

## 0. How to use this pack

1. **Read this master once.** It gives you the shared founder section, the design system, the build checklist and the compliance guard‑rails that apply to *all* ten versions.
2. **Pick (or A/B test) a version `01`–`10`.** Each is a complete, standalone landing‑page blueprint: a different hook, different story, different copy, different visual mood. They are deliberately *not* ten coats of paint on one page — the hero, the "big idea", the emotional driver and the section logic genuinely differ.
3. **Drop in the shared blocks.** Wherever a version says *"Founder block → see Master §4"* or *"Build → see Master §6"*, pull it from here. That is the "keep a few sections common" instruction honoured.
4. **Customise the placeholders.** Anything in `[square brackets]` (years of experience, real student counts, real credentials, real cohort dates) must be filled with **true, verifiable** details. Never invent numbers — in this market, fabricated proof is both a trust‑killer and a legal risk.

> **The single rule that governs every word of copy in this pack:** sell **skill, process and discipline — never income.** No "₹X/day", no profit screenshots, no guaranteed returns, no live buy/sell calls, only data ≥3 months old. This is not just ethics; post‑SEBI it is what keeps you out of court and your ad accounts alive. (Full rationale in your `trading-education-blueprint.md`.)

### 1.2 The recurring "moves" you can mix and match

Across all of them, the same building blocks recur. The 10 versions are different **combinations and emphases** of these:

- **Named methodology** ("5‑Step Cycle", "Trading in the Zone", "Data Science") → makes teaching feel institutional and proprietary. *Give AK its own.*
- **Founder redemption arc** (early loss → discipline) → relatable, honest, disarms skeptics.
- **"What's out there vs us"** contrast → converts the burned trader.
- **Independence promise** ("trade on your own, rely on no one's calls") → the compliant version of empowerment.
- **Free workshop/masterclass funnel** → the universal lead event.
- **Counsellor‑callback close, pricing off‑site** → higher conversion on a higher‑ticket cohort.
- **Vernacular access** (Hindi/Kannada/Bengali) → underserved by English‑only national brands.
- **Trust stack** (credential badge, real student count, video testimonials, third‑party reviews, ISO/NSE/NISM).
- **Honesty as counter‑signal** (admit "9 in 10 lose, no guarantees") → makes everything else believable.

## 2. The compliance frame (governs all 10 versions — read once)

Condensed from your blueprint; this is the guard‑rail every headline and section must pass.

| You CAN say / show | You must NOT say / show |
|---|---|
| Teach concepts, frameworks, technical & fundamental analysis, **psychology, risk management** | Buy/sell/hold **calls**, "targets", stop‑losses on specific live securities |
| Use historical data **≥3 months old** to illustrate | Live/real‑time prices implying a future move |
| Outcome claims about **skill** ("learn to read price action", "build a written plan") | Performance/return claims ("earn ₹X/day", "guaranteed", "double your money") |
| Show *educational* annotated charts (lagged), journals, checklists | Cherry‑picked **profit screenshots / P&L** as proof of a method |
| Sell courses, workshops, memberships, a **learning** community | A disguised advisory ("premium tips group") |
| Display real credentials (NISM RA/IA, NSE cert), real student counts, real reviews | Fabricated numbers, fake countdowns, rented‑Lambo imagery |

**Mandatory on every page:** a visible disclaimer near any claim and in the footer — *"For educational purposes only. Not investment advice. We do not provide buy/sell tips or guarantee returns. Trading in securities is subject to market risk; SEBI data shows ~9 in 10 F&O traders lose money. [SEBI registration status stated honestly.]"* Plus the four policy pages (Refund, Privacy, Terms, Contact). **Strongly recommended:** route the founder through **NISM Series‑XV (Research Analyst)** — it is both your top trust badge and your future‑proofing.

## 5. SHARED DESIGN SYSTEM (the base every version customises)

Each version overrides the **accent colour, type personality and one signature layout motif**. Everything below is the common substrate — keep it consistent so the brand feels coherent no matter which hook you ship.

### 5.1 Global principles (non‑negotiable)
- **Mobile‑first.** 80%+ of Indian traffic is mobile. Design the 360–414px view first; desktop is the enhancement.
- **One primary CTA colour, used *only* for CTAs.** Never paint a non‑clickable element in the CTA colour.
- **Sticky mobile CTA bar** (bottom) on every version: "Register Free →".
- **Trust badge near every CTA** (credential + rating + "no tips, no guarantees").
- **Fast:** target < 2.5s LCP. Compress hero imagery, lazy‑load below the fold, system/variable fonts.
- **Real faces > stock photos.** Especially for the founder and testimonials.
- **Disclaimer** visible in footer and adjacent to any claim.
- **Accessibility:** AA contrast (4.5:1 body text), focus states on all interactive elements, alt text, 44px min tap targets, prefers‑reduced‑motion respected.

### 5.2 Base colour tokens (the AK system, from your current site)
The current mock uses **dark navy + a green accent + white** — a credible, "financial" palette. Keep this as the base; each version shifts the *accent* and *mood*.

```
--ink-900:      #0A1A2F   /* near‑black navy — hero bg, footers */
--ink-700:      #12243B   /* card bg on dark sections */
--ink-500:      #1E3A5F   /* secondary navy */
--paper-0:      #FFFFFF   /* light section bg */
--paper-50:     #F5F8FB   /* alt light bg / cards */
--paper-100:    #E8EEF4   /* borders, dividers */
--text-900:     #0E1726   /* body text on light */
--text-600:     #4A5A6E   /* muted text */
--accent-green: #2ECC71   /* AK base accent — CTAs, highlights */
--accent-green-700:#22A35B/* hover/pressed */
--success:      #2ECC71   /* up moves (use sparingly, educationally) */
--danger:       #E74C3C   /* down moves / honest "loss" stat */
--focus-ring:   #4DA3FF
```
*(Per‑version accent overrides are specified in each file. E.g. `01` leans on `--danger` for the honest loss‑stat; `03` swaps green for an "academic" deep teal/gold; `05` mutes to calm slate/sand; `10` uses a steel/amber "risk" pair.)*

### 5.3 Typography (base)
- **Display / headlines:** a confident grotesque/geometric sans — e.g. **Plus Jakarta Sans**, **Sora**, or **Space Grotesk** (700/800). The current mock's heavy condensed look reads as "bold/decisive".
- **Body:** a highly legible humanist sans — **Inter** or **IBM Plex Sans** (400/500/600). IBM Plex pairs well with a "data/finance" feel.
- **Vernacular:** ensure the chosen body font has **Devanagari** (Hindi) and **Kannada** glyph support, or pair with **Noto Sans Devanagari / Noto Sans Kannada** for vernacular variants (versions 06 & 08 especially).
- **Type scale (mobile → desktop):** H1 32→56, H2 24→40, H3 20→28, body 16→18, small 14. Line‑height 1.15 headlines, 1.6 body.
- *(Each version may swap the display face for personality — noted per file.)*

### 5.4 Spacing, layout, components
- **8‑pt spacing system** (8/16/24/32/48/64/96). Section vertical padding: 64 mobile / 96–120 desktop.
- **Max content width** 1140px; text measure 60–75ch.
- **Cards:** 16px radius, soft shadow (`0 4px 24px rgba(10,26,47,.08)`), 1px `--paper-100` border on light.
- **Buttons:** Primary = filled accent, 14px radius, 52px tall on mobile, bold label + chevron. Secondary = ghost/outline. Always include hover, active, focus, disabled, and loading states.
- **Forms (registration):** fields = Name, WhatsApp (with +91), Email; large labels; inline validation; single primary button; Cloudflare Turnstile for bot protection; success state that confirms WhatsApp‑community join.
- **Icons:** one consistent set (e.g. **Lucide**) — line style, 1.75 stroke.
- **Motion:** subtle, purposeful — 150–250ms ease‑out fades/slides on scroll‑in; never animate the loss‑stat gratuitously; respect reduced‑motion.

### 5.5 Reusable component inventory (build these once, theme per version)
`Sticky mobile CTA bar` · `Hero (headline + sub + CTA + trust line + visual)` · `Trust badge row` · `Stat band (e.g. 2000+ / 50+ / 10+ / 100%)` · `Problem/agitation block` · `Reframe block` · `Named‑methodology diagram` · `Founder block (Master §4)` · `Testimonial card (video + text, skill‑outcome)` · `Curriculum accordion` · `Bonus stack list` · `FAQ accordion` · `Guarantee/risk‑reversal band` · `Final CTA band` · `Footer (links + contact + disclaimer)` · `Registration modal/page + Turnstile` · `Counsellor‑callback confirmation`.

### 6.3 The universal section order (every version follows this skeleton; the *content* is what differs)

## 7. How to A/B test the 10 versions (don't ship all ten)

1. **Choose by psychological distance, not taste.** The biggest learning comes from testing versions that pull different emotional levers. Strong first triads:
   - *Honesty vs Aspiration vs Story:* `01` (Anti‑Loss) · `03` (Life Skill) · `09` (Redemption Story).
   - *Pain‑led vs Identity‑led:* `04` (Anti‑Guru) · `06` (People With Jobs).
   - *Problem‑aware vs Solution‑aware:* `05` (Your Enemy Is You) · `07` (Data, Not Emotion).
2. **Hold everything else constant** — same masterclass, same offer, same traffic source — so the *page* is the only variable.
3. **Decide on funnel metrics, in order:** cost‑per‑registration → show‑up rate → attendee‑to‑buyer → refund rate. A pretty page that doesn't fill the masterclass loses.
4. **Significance over vanity:** give each variant enough registrations to read a real difference (don't call it after 30 visitors). Use Clarity heatmaps to see *where* the losing version drops.
5. **Then iterate within the winner** — swap one hero headline, one piece of proof, at a time.
6. **Refresh every 7–14 days** if running paid; reuse your best‑performing organic video as the ad.
