01 · The honest problem

Be honest — why did your last ten trades happen?

"A friend said."

"It looked like it was going up."

"Someone posted it."

"I had a feeling."

That's not trading — that's guessing with money. And guessing has no edge, no consistency, and no way to improve, because there's nothing to measure.

Every losing trader has the same hidden flaw: no system. No defined rules for what to trade, when to enter, when to exit, or how much to risk. Without that, you're not running a process — you're rolling dice and calling it analysis.

~9 in 10

individual F&O traders lose money

SEBI's data is brutal for a reason: roughly nine in ten individual F&O traders lost money in FY25 — and gut-feel is the common thread. The difference between the nine who lose and the one who doesn't is almost never "a better tip." It's a repeatable process and ruthless risk management.

Source: SEBI study on individual F&O traders, FY25. Cited for education; figures may be revised by the regulator.

02 · The shift

A trade should be the output of a rule, not a feeling.

A real system answers — in advance, in writing — exactly what qualifies as a setup, where you enter, where you exit if you're wrong, where you exit if you're right, and how much you risk. Then you test those rules against historical data to see whether the idea ever actually had an edge, before you risk a rupee.

The gambler

  • Reacts and hopes
  • Trades on tips and feelings
  • Can't explain why a trade happened
  • Nothing to measure, nothing to improve

The professional

  • Runs a tested process
  • Follows written, defined rules
  • Reviews the evidence, refines the system
  • Can defend every decision with data

We teach you to build, test, and run your own. Not a system to copy — a skill you own.

03 · The named method

The Rules-Based Trading System (RTS)

A clear pipeline from idea → tested rules → disciplined execution. Each stage is concrete, written down, and reviewable.

01

Define

Written rules for setup, entry, exit, and risk. No ambiguity.

02

Test

Back-test the rules on historical data to check for a real edge.

03

Size

Position sizing and risk limits as fixed, non-negotiable inputs.

04

Execute

Follow the rules mechanically. Emotion gets no vote.

05

Measure

Journal every trade, analyse the data, refine the rules.

If you can't write your strategy down as rules and test it on past data, you don't have a strategy — you have a hunch. We fix that.

Frequently asked questions

Do I need to be good at maths or coding?
No. "Systematic" means having clear written rules you test — not building algorithms. If you can follow a checklist, you can do this.
Isn't back-testing on old data pointless?
Used honestly, it's how you check whether an idea ever had an edge before you risk money. We teach its uses and its limits — and we only ever use historical data, by design and by law.
Will you give signals or a "system to copy"?
No. We teach you to build and test your own rules, so you're never dependent on anyone. Giving buy/sell calls would also be illegal for an educator.
