### 2 · Problem / Agitation
> "Ask yourself why your last ten trades happened. Be honest. 'A friend said.' 'It looked like it was going up.' 'Someone posted it.' 'I had a feeling.'
>
> That's not trading — that's *guessing with money*. And guessing has no edge, no consistency, and no way to improve, because there's nothing to measure. Every losing trader has the same hidden flaw: **no system.** No defined rules for what to trade, when to enter, when to exit, or how much to risk. Without that, you're not running a process — you're rolling dice and calling it analysis. SEBI's data is brutal for a reason: ~9 in 10 lose, and gut‑feel is the common thread."

### 3 · Reframe / Opportunity
> "Here's the shift: **a trade should be the output of a rule, not a feeling.**
>
> A real system answers — in advance, in writing — exactly what qualifies as a setup, where you enter, where you exit if you're wrong, where you exit if you're right, and how much you risk. Then you test those rules against *historical* data to see whether the idea ever actually had an edge, before you risk a rupee. That's the difference between a professional and a gambler: one runs a tested process and reviews the evidence; the other reacts and hopes. We teach you to build, test, and run your own."

### 4 · Named Methodology — **The Rules‑Based Trading System (RTS)**
**Objective:** Make "systematic" concrete and ownable.

A clear pipeline from idea → tested rules → disciplined execution:
1. **Define** — written rules for setup, entry, exit, and risk (no ambiguity).
2. **Test** — back‑test the rules on **historical** data to check for a real edge.
3. **Size** — position sizing and risk limits as fixed, non‑negotiable inputs.
4. **Execute** — follow the rules mechanically; emotion gets no vote.
5. **Measure** — journal every trade; analyse the data; refine the rules.

> Microcopy: *"If you can't write your strategy down as rules and test it on past data, you don't have a strategy — you have a hunch. We fix that."*

- **Accent strategy:** crisp, "data‑terminal" feel — keep the navy base; introduce a **precise electric‑blue or cyan** accent for data/highlights alongside the green CTA. Monospace numerals reinforce the analytical mood.

## Compliance flags (this version)
- **Back‑testing is the compliance tightrope:** only ever use data **≥3 months old**, label it, and never present a back‑test as a *forward* performance promise ("this returned X, so you'll make X" is forbidden). Frame it strictly as "checking whether an idea had a historical edge".
- "Edge / system" language must not imply guaranteed or expected returns.
- Never provide a copy‑paste "system to follow live" — that edges toward advice. Teach them to build their own.
