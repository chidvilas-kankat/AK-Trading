### 1 · Hero
**Objective:** In 5 seconds, tell the truth and offer the antidote.

- **Eyebrow:** `SEBI study, FY25 · ~91% of F&O traders lost money`
- **H1:** **9 out of 10 traders lose money.**
  **We teach you the discipline of the 1 who doesn't.**
- **Sub:** "A genuinely educational live masterclass on how the market actually works — risk first, process always. No tips. No signals. No income promises. Just the honest skills the small minority of consistent traders rely on."
- **Primary CTA:** `Register Free for the Masterclass →`
- **Secondary (ghost):** `Watch a 2‑min sample lesson`
- **Trust line (under CTA):** `Taught by [Abul Kalam, NISM‑Certified RA] · [N]+ learners · Rated [4.x]★ · No buy/sell tips, ever`
- **Hero visual:** a single, clean "1 in 10" data motif (nine muted figures, one in accent) or an honest equity‑curve‑with‑drawdowns illustration — *not* a green rocket. Restraint signals honesty.

### 2 · Problem / Agitation
**Objective:** Make them feel the real cost of untrained trading (agitate lack of *process*, never to push a trade).

> "You opened a demat account. You watched the reels. You took a few trades on a 'gut feeling' or a tip from a Telegram group — and the account is bleeding.
>
> You're not stupid. You were never taught a *system*. SEBI's own data says about **91% of F&O traders lost money last year** — roughly **₹1.05 lakh crore**, gone, an average of **₹1.1 lakh per person**. The difference between the nine who lose and the one who doesn't is almost never 'a better tip'. It's a repeatable process and ruthless risk management — which nobody ever sat you down and taught. Until now."

### 3 · Reframe / Opportunity
**Objective:** Move from grim truth to credible, non‑hyped possibility.

> "Here's the part the gurus won't tell you, because it doesn't sell dreams: **trading is a learnable skill, not a jackpot.** It's closer to flying a plane than to buying a lottery ticket. Pilots don't 'feel' their way through a flight — they follow checklists and manage risk relentlessly. We teach trading the same way: a defined process, a written plan, position sizing, and the psychology to actually follow your own rules.
>
> We can't promise you profits. Anyone who does is lying or breaking the law. What we *can* do is teach you to think and act like a disciplined trader — and tell you the honest truth about the odds while we do it."

### 4 · Named Methodology — **The "Survive‑First" Framework**
**Objective:** Make the teaching feel institutional and ordered around *not losing*.

Present a simple, ordered framework (your AK equivalent of Akoona's 5‑step cycle), sequenced so **risk comes before reward**:
1. **Survive** — risk rules, position sizing, max‑drawdown limits (taught *first*, on purpose).
2. **See** — read price honestly: trend, support/resistance, structure (historical examples only).
3. **Plan** — a written, rules‑based trading plan; entries, exits, and *when not to trade*.
4. **Sustain** — psychology, journaling, review — the habits the 1‑in‑10 actually keep.

> Microcopy: *"Most courses teach this backwards — strategy first, risk as an afterthought. That's exactly why most students still lose. We flip it."*

### 7 · Curriculum (risk‑led accordion)
**Objective:** Make the value concrete — and notice risk leads.
1. **Why 9 in 10 lose** — the real, structural reasons (and the data behind them).
2. **Risk management first** — position sizing, R‑multiples, max‑drawdown rules.
3. **Reading price honestly** — trend, S/R, structure on historical charts.
4. **Building your trading plan** — entries, exits, rules, and the "do‑not‑trade" list.
5. **Trading psychology** — FOMO, revenge‑trading, and discipline systems.
6. **Journaling & review** — the compounding habit most traders skip.
7. **Putting it together** — a paper‑trading framework; observed, educational practice (no calls).

## Version‑specific UI/UX direction

- **Accent strategy:** this is the one version where the **honest red statistic is a design feature.** Use `--danger #E74C3C` for the "9 out of 10" / "91%" numerals in the hero and stat band; keep `--accent-green` strictly for CTAs. The tension between the red truth and the green action *is* the visual idea.
- **Mood:** editorial and restrained — lots of white space, large type, almost like a serious finance feature article. No rockets, no Lambos, no confetti.
- **Type:** display in **Space Grotesk** 800 (decisive, slightly technical); body **IBM Plex Sans** (its "data" character suits the statistic‑led page).
- **Hero composition:** big H1 left, the "1‑in‑10" data motif right; on mobile, stat motif sits *above* a left‑aligned headline. Animate the nine‑figures motif in *once*, gently (respect reduced‑motion) — never loop it.
- **Stat band:** reframe the shared `2000+ / 50+ / 10+ / 100%` band to *honesty* metrics where true — e.g. `[N]+ learners · [4.x]★ real reviews · 0 tips given · 7‑day refund`.
- **Micro‑interactions:** counters that tick *up* feel celebratory and off‑brand here; prefer numbers that simply appear. The drawdown illustration can draw‑on once.
- **Mobile:** sticky bottom CTA "Register Free →"; the loss‑stat stays visible high on the page; testimonials become a swipe carousel.

## Compliance flags (this version)
- The headline quotes a **real SEBI figure** — keep it accurate and dated ("SEBI study, FY25"). Don't round it into a slogan that overstates.
- "Discipline of the 1" must never tip into implying *you'll* be the 1 (that's a veiled performance claim). Keep it about *the skill the 1 uses*, results‑depend‑on‑you.
- All charts in §4/§7 must be ≥3 months old and clearly educational.
