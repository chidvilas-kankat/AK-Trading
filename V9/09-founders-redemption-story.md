### 4 · Named Methodology — **The Rebuild Method**
**Objective:** turn the personal arc into a transferable system — the five things AK had to change, in the exact order he changed them.

The same five shifts that turned losing into discipline:
1. **Accept the math.** Most traders lose. I stopped pretending I was the exception and started respecting the odds.
2. **Kill the ego.** No more tips, no more "I just know". Conviction without a plan is how I blew up.
3. **Rules over feelings.** Every entry, exit and size decided *in advance* — so the scared or greedy version of me couldn't override the calm one.
4. **Risk before reward.** Decide what I'm willing to lose *before* I think about what I might gain. Protect the downside first, always.
5. **Process over outcome.** Judge each trade by whether I followed my rules, not by whether it paid — because good process is the only thing that survives a bad week.

> Microcopy: *"This isn't theory I read somewhere. It's the exact order in which I had to fix myself. We teach it the same way."*

## Version‑specific UI/UX direction

- **Accent strategy:** the most **editorial / magazine** look in the pack. Let the navy base recede into a warm off‑white reading background; use the green sparingly as an accent on the timeline and CTAs. The feeling is *long‑form journalism*, not a sales page — restraint signals sincerity.
- **Signature motif: the timeline.** A single vertical (desktop: could be a horizontal band in the hero) line that runs the page — *[year] the loss → the rebuild → certified → today* — with the story beats pinned to it. This is the one visual device unique to v09; make it beautiful and central.
- **Type:** an **editorial serif** for headlines to signal "story / publication" — **Fraunces**, **Spectral**, or **Source Serif 4** — paired with a clean **Inter / IBM Plex Sans** body. This is the *only* version in the pack that should use a serif display face; it's a deliberate signal that this page is a story, not a pitch.
- **Hero composition:** a large, authentic, slightly imperfect founder portrait (warm, real, eye‑contact) beside the one‑line confession. Absolutely no luxury props, no rented cars, no stacks of cash — those would destroy the credibility the whole page depends on.
- **Imagery:** documentary‑style and human throughout — the founder teaching, real (consented) student moments, the desk, the books. Photography over icons. Black‑and‑white or muted treatments reinforce the editorial tone.
- **Pull‑quotes:** lift the most honest lines ("none of it had been skill") into large pull‑quotes between sections, magazine‑style, to keep the long read moving.
- **Micro‑interactions:** the timeline fills/illuminates as the reader scrolls through the story — a quiet sense of progression from the fall to today. Keep motion subtle and dignified.
- **Mobile:** the timeline becomes a clean vertical spine down the left edge; sticky "Hear the Full Story →"; keep generous line‑height and a comfortable reading measure — this version is *read*, not skimmed.

## Compliance flags (this version)
- **The single biggest risk in the whole pack lives here:** the redemption arc must resolve into *discipline and survival*, **never** "…and then I made it all back / got rich / became profitable." Any "from broke to crorepati" implication is an income claim and is out. Keep the transformation strictly behavioural (gambler → disciplined, reckless → risk‑aware).
- **Only true statements.** Do not fabricate or exaggerate the loss for drama. If AK's genuine story is milder, tell the milder truth. A real, modest story beats an invented dramatic one on both ethics and persuasion.
- No profit screenshots, P&L, or "look how I recovered" evidence — even as proof of the comeback. The proof on this page is *credibility and method*, not money.
- Keep ~9‑in‑10‑lose framing and the no‑tips/no‑signals stance explicit, exactly as in the other versions; the emotional warmth of the story makes prominent disclaimers *more* important, not less.
- All teaching examples ≥3 months old and clearly educational.
